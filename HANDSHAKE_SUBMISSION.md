## One-line tagline (for the card)

## Primary blurb (~120 words)

air.grid is an interactive map that puts three things side by side for the first time: where U.S.
industries emit, what the air actually measures right now, and the schools, campuses, and
neighborhoods sitting next to both. [I built it because air-quality and emissions data exist but
live scattered across half a dozen federal portals — each with its own format and rate limit — and
I couldn't find a single place to just answer "what's in the air around my campus?"] It pulls live
sensor data from AirNow, PurpleAir, and OpenAQ alongside EPA emissions and Census data, renders 10k+
points at 60fps, and refreshes hourly. I built it with a multi-agent Claude Code workflow —
parallel data-ingestion agents coordinating through a shared schema contract.
